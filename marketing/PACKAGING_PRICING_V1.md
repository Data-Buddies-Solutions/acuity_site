# Acuity Health Packaging + Pricing V1

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how Acuity Health should package, name, and price its offering so the company presents as one coherent platform rather than a collection of disconnected tools.

The goal is to align product packaging with the broader category strategy:

- `Acuity = patient access and engagement for eye care`
- `Wedge = AI-native front desk`
- `Expansion = phone system + patient engagement + workflow modules`

## 2. Core Strategic Principle

Do not present Acuity as three unrelated products.

Present Acuity as:

`the AI-native communications and workflow layer for private practices, starting with the front desk`

For eye care specifically:

`Acuity helps practices answer every patient, modernize the phone layer, and keep communication moving across calls, texts, reminders, routing, scheduling, and follow-up.`

## 3. Product Architecture

### Umbrella Brand

`Acuity Platform`

This is the master product story. It owns the full communication layer between the practice and the patient.

### Core Product Groups

#### 1. Acuity Access

This is the primary wedge offer and should be the main public-facing entry point.

Purpose:

- answer inbound demand
- reduce front-desk overload
- route and schedule patients correctly
- modernize the front door of the practice

Modules inside Acuity Access:

- `Acuity AI Receptionist`
- `Acuity Voice`

#### 2. Acuity Engage

This is the expansion offer that takes Acuity from patient access into patient engagement.

Purpose:

- keep patients informed
- improve confirmations and follow-up
- support texting and shared inbox workflows
- extend communication beyond the live call

### Add-On Layer

Productized upgrades that multiple practices may want.

Examples:

- transcripts and call summaries
- call recording
- advanced reporting
- outbound voice
- recall and reactivation campaigns
- extra numbers
- advanced triaging

### Custom Work Layer

Reserved for true one-off engineering.

Examples:

- EMR note upload
- custom API actions
- unique integration logic
- highly practice-specific automation

Rule:

Configuration is not custom work.
Custom work should be rare, premium, scoped separately, and never become the default sales motion.

## 4. Naming Recommendation

### Public-Facing Naming

Keep the website and marketing simple:

- `Acuity Access`
- `Acuity Engage`
- `Enterprise / Multi-Location`

### Internal / Sales Naming

Use the more detailed module naming for proposals, pricing, and configuration:

- `Acuity AI Receptionist`
- `Acuity Voice`
- `Acuity Engage`
- add-ons
- custom work

### Naming to Avoid

Avoid these in public-facing positioning:

- `VoIP Call Center`
- `AI automation services`
- `custom dev shop`
- generic `medical AI platform`

Why:

- `VoIP Call Center` sounds commoditized
- `AI automation services` sounds like an agency
- `custom dev shop` weakens repeatability

## 6. Recommended Packaging by Module

### A. Acuity AI Receptionist

Purpose:

- own inbound patient communication
- remove repetitive phone burden
- improve booking and routing

Includes:

- inbound AI call handling
- FAQ handling
- EMR scheduling
- staff transfer workflows
- after-hours coverage
- whisper

Recommended pricing structure:

- implementation fee
- monthly platform fee
- usage-based AI minute fee

Reason:

Usage-only pricing makes the product feel like labor metering.
A monthly platform fee better reflects software value, workflow configuration, support, reliability, analytics, and integration maintenance.

### B. Acuity Voice

Purpose:

- provide the modern phone system underneath the front desk experience

Includes:

- 1 business number
- 5 transfer numbers
- staff calling
- missed calls
- voicemail
- ring groups and queues
- transfers
- basic dashboard

Recommended role in the offer:

- sold as infrastructure beneath Acuity Access
- can be sold standalone if needed
- should usually support AI Receptionist rather than compete with it conceptually

### C. Acuity Engage

Purpose:

- extend patient communication into reminders, texting, and follow-up

Includes:

- 2-way texting
- appointment reminders
- confirmation texts
- thank-you texts
- missed-call text back
- shared inbox

Recommended role in the offer:

- natural upsell after Access
- bridge from patient access into patient engagement

## 7. Pricing Logic

Pricing should use one consistent stack across products.

### Standard Pricing Stack

#### 1. Implementation Fee

Covers:

- onboarding
- workflow configuration
- routing setup
- templates
- scheduling logic
- integration setup
- go-live support

#### 2. Monthly Platform Fee

Covers:

- software access
- reporting
- workflow management
- maintenance
- support
- platform value independent of raw usage

#### 3. Usage-Based Pricing

Covers variable consumption such as:

- AI voice minutes
- text volume
- telephony overages

#### 4. Add-On Pricing

Covers productized upgrades that extend the platform.

#### 5. Custom Work Fee

Covers scoped engineering outside the standard platform.

## 8. Recommended Pricing Framework

These are structural recommendations, not final published pricing commitments.

### Acuity AI Receptionist

#### Implementation

- 1 location: `$10,000`
- 2-3 locations: `$18,000-$25,000`
- 4-6 locations: `$30,000-$40,000`

#### Monthly Platform Fee

Recommended to add.

Example structure:

- 1 location: `$750-$1,500/month`
- multi-location: custom tiered pricing based on practice size and complexity

#### Usage

- `$0.21 per AI minute`

#### Recommended add-ons

- transcripts and call summaries
- EMR note upload
- advanced workflow automation

### Acuity Voice

#### Monthly plans

- Starter: `$300/month` includes `15,000 minutes`
- Standard: `$450/month` includes `30,000 minutes`
- Pro: `$600/month` includes `50,000 minutes`

#### Overage

- additional minutes: `$0.015/min`
- additional number: `$2/month`

#### Add-ons

- call recording
- advanced reporting
- advanced triaging

### Acuity Engage

#### Monthly plans

- Starter: `$199/month` includes `5,000 texts`
- Growth: `$299/month` includes `10,000 texts`
- Pro: `$499/month` includes `20,000 texts`

#### Overage

- additional texts: `$0.015/text` plus carrier fees where applicable

#### Add-ons

- outbound appointment calls
- recall / reactivation campaigns
- custom text automation

## 9. Public vs Private Pricing Recommendation

### Public-Facing Website

Keep pricing simple and controlled.

Recommended public approach:

- `Starts with implementation + monthly platform + usage`
- highlight example ranges only if useful
- invite practices to book a demo for a tailored quote

Why:

- implementation complexity varies by location count and workflow depth
- too much detail on the site may create confusion
- detailed pricing works better in sales conversations and proposals

### Sales / Internal Pricing

Use detailed pricing sheets with:

- implementation ranges
- monthly base fees
- usage assumptions
- add-ons
- custom work rules

## 11. Sales Positioning Rules

### Positioning to Use

- `communications layer`
- `patient access`
- `patient engagement`
- `front desk modernization`
- `workflow-native`

### Positioning to Avoid

- `custom AI development`
- `agency`

- `generic call center`

Start with:

- missed calls
- front-desk burden
- after-hours demand
- patient responsiveness

Then expand into:

- texting
- reminders
- follow-up
- workflow automation

Do not start with:

- infrastructure
- implementation details
- technical architecture

## 12. Expansion Path

The default growth path for accounts should be:

1. `Start with Acuity Access`
2. `Add Acuity Engage`
3. `Add modules and reporting`
4. `Add custom work only when strategically justified`

This keeps the business productized while still allowing expansion revenue.

## 13. Internal Rules for Deal Discipline

### Included Configuration

Included in standard implementation:

- routing rules
- hours
- escalation logic
- templates
- practice settings
- standard workflow setup

### Productized Add-Ons

Increase monthly recurring revenue.

Only add items here if they are repeatable across practices.

### Custom Work

Use only when:

- engineering is truly required
- the feature is not yet productized
- the work cannot be scoped as a standard module

Custom work should:

- carry a separate one-time fee
- be documented in writing
- be evaluated for productization later

## 14. Final Positioning Summary

### Category

`Patient access and engagement for eye care`

### Platform Story

`Acuity is the AI-native communications and workflow layer for private practices, starting with the front desk.`

### Offer Story

- `Acuity Access` captures and routes patient demand
- `Acuity Engage` keeps patients informed and responsive
- together they form the communication layer between practice and patient

### Strategic Recommendation

Market the platform simply.
Sell the modules intelligently.
Keep custom work constrained.
Use pricing that reflects software value, not just usage volume.
