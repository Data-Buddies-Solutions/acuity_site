# Acuity Health Marketing Strategy V1

## 1. Strategic Thesis

Acuity Health should not market itself primarily as an `AI receptionist company`.
That is the mechanism, not the category.

The category to build is:

`patient access and engagement for ophthalmology and optometry practices`

The wedge into that category is:

`answer every patient call, reduce front-desk overload, and convert more demand into booked visits`

The long-term brand position is:

`Acuity Health is the patient communication layer for modern eye care practices.`

## 2. Core Market Insight

Eye care practices do not mainly feel they have an "AI problem."
They feel they have:

- missed calls
- front-desk overload
- inconsistent patient communication
- after-hours leakage
- difficulty scaling without adding admin burden

So the product should be framed as a solution to `operational strain and patient access friction`, not as advanced technology.

## 3. Theory Foundation

### Jobs to Be Done

Practices hire Acuity to:

- answer every patient inquiry
- reduce phone bottlenecks
- book and confirm appointments
- handle repetitive communication consistently
- let staff focus on in-office care

### Behavioral Economics

Key buyer triggers:

- `loss aversion`: every missed call can mean lost revenue and lost lifetime patient value
- `risk reduction`: reliable, consistent call handling feels safer than novelty
- `cognitive ease`: simple operational outcomes outperform technical explanations
- `social proof`: practices trust what peers in their specialty already use

### Sociology

Ophthalmology and optometry practices are:

- reputation-sensitive
- operationally strained
- medically serious
- staff-constrained
- highly aware that patient trust begins before the visit

Messaging must preserve their identity.
The brand should feel calm, competent, and practice-aware, not startup-hypey.

### Psychology of Adoption

Adoption rises when the product feels:

- familiar
- controllable
- low-risk
- staff-supportive rather than staff-threatening

So the product should be positioned as:

`a more responsive front door for the practice`

not:

`a replacement for your front desk`

## 4. Category Definition

Acuity's category is:

`AI-powered patient access and engagement for eye care`

Definition:

Patient access and engagement means making the practice easy to reach, easy to schedule with, easy to understand, and easy to return to.

This category is broader and stronger than `AI receptionist`, but still concrete enough to sell.

## 5. Positioning Statement

Acuity Health helps ophthalmology and optometry practices answer every patient call, reduce front-desk overload, and improve patient engagement through AI-powered voice reception and modern phone systems built specifically for eye care workflows.

## 6. Ideal Customer Profile

Best-fit customers:

- independent ophthalmology groups
- optometry practices with high inbound call volume
- multi-location eye care practices
- practices struggling with hold times, voicemail, staffing pressure, or after-hours demand
- practices that care about professionalism, patient experience, and operational efficiency

Strong signals of fit:

- missed or abandoned calls
- heavy scheduling volume
- multilingual patient population
- front-desk burnout
- growth goals constrained by communication bottlenecks

## 7. Buyer Map

### Physician owner / partner

Cares about:

- growth
- reputation
- patient experience
- operational leverage
- not losing patients before they are seen

### Practice administrator / office manager

Cares about:

- staffing pressure
- workflow breakdowns
- call handling efficiency
- escalation quality
- predictable operations

### Front-desk lead

Cares about:

- fewer repetitive interruptions
- smoother handoffs
- less chaos
- clarity on when humans step in
- not being displaced or blamed

## 8. Core Pains to Own

Acuity should aim to own these problems in the market's mind:

- missed calls are missed patients
- voicemail kills conversion
- front desks are overloaded with repetitive phone work
- after-hours demand is being lost
- inconsistent communication weakens trust
- patient experience starts with the first call, not the first visit

## 9. Messaging Pillars

### Patient Access

Every patient can reach the practice without hold-time friction, dead ends, or voicemail bottlenecks.

### Front-Desk Relief

Acuity removes repetitive call load so staff can focus on higher-value patient interactions.

### Revenue Protection

More answered calls and more completed scheduling means less leakage and better capture of demand.

### Patient Engagement

Consistent, professional, multilingual communication improves trust and keeps patients moving through the care journey.

### Eye Care Specialization

Built for ophthalmology and optometry workflows, not generic healthcare or generic call centers.

## 10. Brand Promise

`Acuity helps eye care practices become easier to reach, easier to schedule with, and easier to trust.`

## 11. Brand Voice

The voice should be:

- calm
- precise
- operational
- credible
- practice-aware
- modern without sounding futuristic

Avoid:

- generic AI hype
- exaggerated disruption language
- "replace your staff" framing
- broad healthcare jargon

Prefer:

- concrete operational language
- patient-flow language
- measured claims
- serious tone with simple explanations

## 12. Website Direction

Current site strength:
It already communicates niche focus, operational pain, and ROI.

Main website shift:
Move from `AI receptionist` framing to `patient access and engagement` framing, while keeping call-handling as the concrete entry point.

Recommended homepage message:

Headline:

`The patient access and engagement platform for eye care practices`

Subhead:

`Answer every patient call, reduce front-desk overload, and keep scheduling, confirmations, and follow-up moving without hold times or voicemail bottlenecks.`

Key website priorities:

- clarify category
- make the patient journey visible
- increase proof density
- expand beyond claims into examples, workflows, and outcomes

Priority pages:

- homepage
- platform / packaging page
- patient access page
- patient engagement page
- proof or case study page
- ROI / missed-calls calculator page

## 13. Content Strategy

Content should build authority around the category, not just the product.

Three core content pillars:

- `Revenue leakage in eye care`
- `Front-desk operations and staffing strain`
- `Patient access and engagement strategy`

Topics to own:

- missed calls
- after-hours demand
- abandoned bookings
- multilingual patient communication
- recall and confirmation workflows
- why patient engagement begins before the exam room
- how phone systems affect conversion and trust

Content rule:
Each piece should target one buyer and one pain point.

## 14. LinkedIn Strategy

LinkedIn should position Acuity as the voice of modern patient access in eye care.

Primary objective:

Make Acuity synonymous with better patient communication for ophthalmology and optometry.

Content mix:

- 40% category education
- 30% proof and evidence
- 20% point of view
- 10% product

Recurring post themes:

- missed calls are a growth problem
- front-desk overload is a design problem, not just a staffing problem
- patient engagement starts at first contact
- generic call centers do not understand eye care workflows
- operational responsiveness is part of patient trust

## 15. AI Agent Role

The AI agent should own `content operations`, not final strategy.

The agent can own:

- editorial calendar creation
- topic ideation from messaging pillars
- post drafting
- blog drafting
- repurposing site content into social
- comment and reply drafts
- analytics summaries
- performance reporting by topic and buyer resonance

The founder or team should own:

- final approval
- claims validation
- category decisions
- customer truth
- case-study accuracy
- tone calibration

## 16. 90-Day Execution Priorities

### Phase 1: Foundation

- finalize this messaging document
- define category language and homepage hierarchy
- create proof inventory: metrics, customer stories, workflows, screenshots, objections

### Phase 2: Website

- rewrite homepage around patient access and engagement
- create at least 3 high-intent pages
- tighten CTA around live demo and ROI review

### Phase 3: Content

- publish 3 LinkedIn posts per week
- publish 1 buyer-relevant article or insight per week
- publish 1 proof asset or case insight every 2 weeks

### Phase 4: AI Agent SOP

- set topic inputs
- define post templates
- define approval process
- define weekly reporting
- optimize around demo-driving topics

## 17. Key Strategic Principle

Do not lead with `AI`.
Lead with `patient access`, `patient engagement`, and `operational responsiveness`.

AI is the reason the product works.
It is not the deepest reason buyers care.
