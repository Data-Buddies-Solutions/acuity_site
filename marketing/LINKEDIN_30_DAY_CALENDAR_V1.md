# Acuity Health 30-Day LinkedIn Calendar V1

## 1. Objective

This calendar turns Acuity's positioning into a repeatable founder-led LinkedIn system.

The goal is not generic visibility.
The goal is to make Acuity synonymous with:

- patient access for eye care
- front-desk relief
- patient engagement before the visit
- practical AI that protects revenue and reduces operational strain

## 2. Publishing Rules

### Weekly cadence

- `3 posts per week`
- `5 comments per week on relevant industry posts`
- `1 weekly analytics review`

### Content mix

- `40% category education`
- `30% proof and evidence`
- `20% point of view`
- `10% product`

### Primary audiences

- physician owner / partner
- practice administrator / office manager
- front-desk lead

### Voice rules

- calm
- operational
- sharp
- eye-care-specific
- no AI hype
- no startup clichés

## 3. Post Framework

Each post should include:

- one audience
- one pain point
- one sharp point of view
- one operational implication
- one simple CTA

Preferred CTAs:

- `Curious how this looks in a real practice?`
- `We built Acuity to solve exactly this problem.`
- `If this is happening in your practice, it’s worth measuring.`
- `Happy to show how we think about it.`

## 4. Weekly Operating Rhythm

### Monday

Founder point of view or category education

### Wednesday

Operational insight, proof, or benchmark

### Friday

Customer pain, workflow example, or product framing

### Tuesday and Thursday

Comment on posts from:

- practice growth consultants
- ophthalmology operators
- healthcare IT people
- healthcare private equity / MSO operators
- patient experience voices

### Friday afternoon

Review:

- profile visits
- comments from target buyers
- saves
- demo intent
- topic resonance

## 5. 30-Day Calendar

## Week 1

### Day 1

Topic:
`Patient engagement starts before the visit`

Audience:
physician owner / administrator

Format:
text post

Hook:
`Most practices think patient engagement starts with the exam room. It starts with whether a patient can reach you.`

Angle:
Explain that hold times, voicemail, and transfer friction shape trust before care begins.

CTA:
`If a patient cannot get through, the engagement problem started before they arrived.`

### Day 3

Topic:
`Missed calls are revenue leakage`

Audience:
physician owner

Format:
text post with simple math

Hook:
`A missed call is not just a missed call. It is often a lost appointment, a lost procedure, and a patient who never calls back.`

Angle:
Use simple economics around call volume, booking rate, and average visit value.

CTA:
`This is one of the first numbers every practice should measure.`

### Day 5

Topic:
`Front-desk overload is a system design problem`

Audience:
practice administrator

Format:
text post

Hook:
`The front desk is doing high-value human work and low-value repetitive work at the same time. That is the real bottleneck.`

Angle:
Show how repetitive phone tasks dilute in-office care and create chaos.

CTA:
`The fix is not just more staffing. It is better workflow design.`

## Week 2

### Day 8

Topic:
`Why generic call centers fail eye care`

Audience:
administrator / physician owner

Format:
point-of-view post

Hook:
`Eye care practices do not need generic call handling. They need patient access designed around actual appointment types, insurance rules, and escalation logic.`

Angle:
Contrast specialty workflows with generic answering services.

CTA:
`Specialty operations deserve specialty communication systems.`

### Day 10

Topic:
`After-hours demand is a hidden growth channel`

Audience:
physician owner

Format:
text post with metric

Hook:
`A meaningful share of patient demand comes in after the front desk goes home.`

Angle:
Explain why evenings, weekends, and peak overflow periods are often ignored but valuable.

CTA:
`If your practice is unavailable when patients are ready, someone else gets the booking.`

### Day 12

Topic:
`What a modern front door for eye care looks like`

Audience:
administrator / front-desk lead

Format:
workflow post

Hook:
`A modern front door is not a nicer phone tree. It is instant access, intelligent routing, and fewer dead ends.`

Angle:
Describe the desired patient journey from first call to confirmed visit.

CTA:
`That is the standard practices should be working toward.`

## Week 3

### Day 15

Topic:
`What we mean by patient access`

Audience:
all three buyers

Format:
category education

Hook:
`Patient access sounds abstract until you define it operationally.`

Angle:
Define patient access as reachability, scheduling, routing, and responsiveness.

CTA:
`If it is hard to reach the practice, access is broken.`

### Day 17

Topic:
`Staff relief and patient experience are the same problem`

Audience:
administrator / front-desk lead

Format:
text post

Hook:
`Practices often separate staff burnout from patient experience. Operationally, they are the same issue.`

Angle:
Show how overloaded staff create slower, less consistent communication.

CTA:
`Reduce repetitive load and both sides improve.`

### Day 19

Topic:
`A simple benchmark every practice should calculate`

Audience:
physician owner / administrator

Format:
numbers post

Hook:
`Here is a simple benchmark: calls per day x missed-call rate x booking rate x average visit value.`

Angle:
Encourage readers to estimate monthly revenue at risk.

CTA:
`You do not need perfect data to see whether the problem is material.`

## Week 4

### Day 22

Topic:
`Why we package Acuity as Access + Engage`

Audience:
operators and buyers

Format:
founder/product post

Hook:
`We do not think of the phone, text reminders, and follow-up as separate problems. They are one communication system.`

Angle:
Explain the `Acuity Access` and `Acuity Engage` framing.

CTA:
`Start with access. Expand into engagement.`

### Day 24

Topic:
`What patients actually want when they call`

Audience:
front-desk lead / administrator

Format:
patient-experience post

Hook:
`Most patients are not asking for delight. They are asking for speed, clarity, and competence.`

Angle:
Discuss immediate pickup, fewer transfers, and clear next steps.

CTA:
`Responsiveness is one of the most underpriced trust signals in healthcare.`

### Day 26

Topic:
`What good escalation looks like`

Audience:
front-desk lead / administrator

Format:
workflow post

Hook:
`Automation should never trap the patient. Good escalation means the human steps in with context, not confusion.`

Angle:
Talk about transfer logic, context handoff, and keeping patients from repeating themselves.

CTA:
`That is where many systems fail.`

## Week 5

### Day 29

Topic:
`The metric that matters after deployment`

Audience:
administrator / physician owner

Format:
proof post

Hook:
`The first question after deployment is not whether the AI sounds impressive. It is whether the operating metrics changed.`

Angle:
List what should improve in the first 30 to 90 days.

CTA:
`If the metrics do not move, the workflow is not good enough yet.`

### Day 30

Topic:
`Acuity summary post`

Audience:
all buyers

Format:
offer-positioning post

Hook:
`Acuity exists to help eye care practices answer every patient and keep communication moving.`

Angle:
Summarize Access, Engage, and the operational thesis.

CTA:
`If patient access is becoming a bottleneck in your practice, that is exactly what we built for.`
