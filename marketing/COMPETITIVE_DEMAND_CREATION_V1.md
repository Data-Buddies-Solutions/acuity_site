# Acuity Health Competitive Demand Creation V1

## 1. Purpose

This document defines how Acuity should create demand in a market where broader competitors already talk about:

- patient engagement
- phones
- texting
- scheduling
- reviews
- automation

The goal is not to out-list competitor features.
The goal is to create demand around a sharper market belief that makes Acuity feel more relevant, more specialized, and more necessary.

## 2. Market Context

Current competitors like Weave and Aloha are both operating in healthcare communication and engagement, but they frame the category differently.

### Weave

Weave appears to position around:

- all-in-one practice communications
- phones, texting, reviews, scheduling, payments, and more
- broad healthcare and eyecare use cases
- better patient experiences through a unified platform

Observed strategic style:

- broad suite
- operational convenience
- call context and staff enablement
- multi-feature consolidation

### Aloha

Aloha appears to position around:

- AI-powered patient engagement
- online scheduling, texting, digital forms, reviews, automation, and AI receptionist
- specialty landing pages with strong growth language
- practice growth and conversion through communication automation

Observed strategic style:

- growth-led
- engagement and marketing
- lighter-weight entry point
- strong optometry / chiropractic emphasis

## 3. Competitive Interpretation

### What Weave Owns

Weave is strongest when the buyer wants:

- one broad system
- established suite credibility
- multiple office functions under one vendor
- phones and patient interaction tools in one place

### What Aloha Owns

Aloha is strongest when the buyer wants:

- a practice-growth story
- patient engagement automation
- review generation
- online scheduling and front-end conversion
- a more marketing-forward system

### What Neither Owns Cleanly

The strongest whitespace for Acuity is:

`ophthalmology-specific patient engagement built around front-desk responsiveness`

That is the space where Acuity can be sharper than both.

## 4. Acuity's Demand Creation Thesis

The market should learn this belief:

`Patient engagement starts before the visit, with whether the practice is reachable, responsive, and easy to schedule with.`

That is Acuity's demand-creation wedge.

This is important because it reframes patient engagement away from:

- reminder software
- generic texting
- review generation
- broad communication tooling

and toward:

- real responsiveness
- front-desk transformation
- missed-call elimination
- after-hours demand capture
- ophthalmology workflow complexity

## 5. Acuity's Strategic Position

### Category

`Patient engagement for eye care practices`

### Sub-definition

For Acuity, patient engagement means:

- patients can reach the practice
- patients get helped without dead ends
- scheduling and routing work correctly
- reminders and follow-up stay consistent
- the practice feels responsive from first contact onward

### Competitive Position

`Weave = broad suite`

`Aloha = growth and engagement automation`

`Acuity = ophthalmology engagement infrastructure`

That is the position.

## 6. The Beliefs Acuity Should Teach

Demand creation should teach the market these beliefs repeatedly.

### Belief 1

`Patient engagement starts before the visit.`

Meaning:

- if the patient cannot get through
- if they hit voicemail
- if they get bounced between staff
- if they cannot schedule clearly

then engagement is already broken.

### Belief 2

`The front desk is one of the biggest drivers of patient trust and growth.`

Meaning:

- responsiveness is not just an ops issue
- it is a patient experience issue
- and a revenue issue

### Belief 3

`Generic communication systems do not handle ophthalmology complexity well enough.`

Meaning:

- pediatric workflows matter
- medical and vision insurance complexity matters
- multilingual patient communication matters
- high-volume call spikes matter

### Belief 4

`Missed calls are patient engagement failures, not just phone problems.`

Meaning:

- every dropped patient interaction damages trust
- and often destroys booking intent

### Belief 5

`Better patient engagement is measurable.`

Meaning:

- missed calls
- appointments booked
- after-hours calls answered
- staff hours returned
- multilingual booking handled

These are all measurable.

## 7. Demand Creation Pillars

These are the major themes Acuity should own in content and sales messaging.

### 1. Responsiveness

Make the practice easier to reach and easier to choose.

### 2. Front-Desk Relief

Reduce repetitive phone work so staff can focus on patient-facing care.

### 3. Engagement Before the Visit

Engagement begins with first contact, not with the exam room.

### 4. Ophthalmology Complexity

Acuity is built for the real workflows eye care practices deal with every day.

### 5. Measurable Outcomes

Engagement should produce operational and financial improvements, not just nicer messaging.

## 8. Competitive Messaging Strategy

### Against Weave

Do not say:

- "We have more features"
- "We are also all-in-one"

Instead say:

- `Broad suite platforms are useful, but ophthalmology practices still need a communication layer built around specialty complexity and front-desk responsiveness.`

Winning angle:

- more specialty-specific
- more engagement-through-responsiveness
- less generic suite language

### Against Aloha

Do not say:

- "We also do patient engagement"
- "We also have AI"

Instead say:

- `Growth and engagement automation matter, but in ophthalmology the highest-leverage engagement problem is still whether the practice answers, routes, and books patients correctly at scale.`

Winning angle:

- more operational depth
- more ophthalmology-specific complexity
- stronger front-desk transformation story

## 9. Acuity's Strongest Competitive Proof

The best current proof asset supports this position directly:

- 6-location ophthalmology practice
- pediatric doctors
- medical and vision insurance complexity
- 0 missed calls
- 100+ concurrent calls handled
- 2,000+ after-hours calls answered
- 400 staff hours returned
- 500+ appointments booked per month
- full Spanish answering and booking
- robocall filtering

Why this matters strategically:

This is not proof of abstract AI.
This is proof of:

- scale
- specialty complexity
- operational responsiveness
- measurable patient engagement outcomes

## 10. What Acuity Should Avoid in Marketing

Avoid these frames:

- generic healthcare AI
- broad feature menu comparisons
- "all-in-one platform" as the lead message
- over-indexing on reviews / marketing automation as the core story
- vague patient-experience claims without operational proof

These dilute the competitive advantage.
