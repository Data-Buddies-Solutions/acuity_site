# Acuity Health Marketing Operating System V1

## 1. Purpose

Acuity Health exists to become the category leader in `patient access and engagement for eye care practices`.

The operating goal is:

- make Acuity synonymous with better patient communication in ophthalmology and optometry
- turn that position into qualified demos
- build a repeatable content system an AI agent can support

## 2. Core Message Hierarchy

Use this order everywhere:

- Category: `patient access and engagement for eye care`
- Problem: missed calls, front-desk overload, voicemail leakage, after-hours demand, inconsistent patient communication
- Outcome: more answered calls, more booked visits, less admin burden, better patient experience
- Mechanism: AI voice receptionist + modern phone system + EMR-connected workflows
- Proof: specialty focus, HIPAA, integrations, multilingual support, time saved, revenue protected

Never lead with:

- generic AI hype
- "replace your staff"
- broad healthcare automation language

## 3. Audience Map

### Physician owner / partner

- cares about growth, reputation, patient experience, operational leverage
- message: `Acuity protects demand and improves the front door of the practice`

### Practice administrator / office manager

- cares about staffing pressure, workflow reliability, call burden
- message: `Acuity reduces front-desk chaos and creates more predictable operations`

### Front-desk lead

- cares about interruptions, escalations, handoffs, job security
- message: `Acuity removes repetitive phone work so your team can focus on patients who need a human`

## 4. Messaging Pillars

### Patient Access

Every patient can reach the practice without hold-time friction or voicemail dead ends.

### Front-Desk Relief

Repetitive calls stop consuming the team's day.

### Revenue Protection

Missed calls become captured appointments and retained patients.

### Patient Engagement

Scheduling, confirmations, reminders, and follow-up feel consistent and professional.

### Eye-Care Specialization

Built for ophthalmology and optometry workflows, not generic call handling.

## 5. Proof System

Marketing should not run on claims alone.
Build these assets next:

- 1 case study with before/after metrics
- 1 ROI one-pager
- 1 "how Acuity works in your practice" visual
- 3 customer quotes
- 5 screenshots or workflow visuals
- 1 short live demo clip
- 1 objection-handling FAQ for sales and content

Proof points to collect:

- missed calls reduced
- booking rate improvement
- staff hours saved
- after-hours calls captured
- patient satisfaction improvement
- transfer/escalation rate
- setup time

## 7. LinkedIn and Content System

Content objective:

- educate the market
- shape the category
- lower adoption risk
- create demo intent

Content mix:

- 40% category education
- 30% proof and evidence
- 20% point of view
- 10% product

Core content pillars:

- missed calls and revenue leakage
- front-desk overload
- patient engagement before the visit
- after-hours demand capture
- multilingual patient communication
- why generic call handling fails eye care

Posting cadence:

- 3 LinkedIn posts per week
- 1 article / insight per week
- 5 comments per week on relevant industry posts
- 1 proof asset every 2 weeks

## 8. AI Agent Responsibilities

The AI agent should own:

- topic ideation from messaging pillars
- post drafts
- article outlines and first drafts
- repurposing site copy into social/email
- comment/reply drafts
- weekly content calendar
- performance summaries

The human should own:

- final claims
- customer truth
- final publish approval
- strategic shifts
- case study accuracy
- voice/taste decisions

Agent workflow:

- input: sales notes, objections, calls, website messaging, proof artifacts
- process: cluster pain points, assign audience, generate angles
- output: posts, articles, snippets, email copy, follow-up content
- report: top themes, demo-driving topics, objections surfacing most often

## 9. KPIs

Primary metrics:

- qualified demos booked
- demo-to-opportunity rate
- inbound attributed to content
- conversion from high-intent pages

Secondary metrics:

- LinkedIn profile visits
- comments from target buyers
- saves/shares by relevant operators
- time on page
- CTA clickthrough

Do not optimize for vanity reach alone.
